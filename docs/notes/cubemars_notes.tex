\section{V3.0 CAN protocol}
\label{sec:v3}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{Unified extended-ID scheme}

All V3.0 control modes use CAN 2.0B extended (29-bit) frames.
The arbitration ID encodes both the packet type and the motor's ESC\_ID:
\begin{equation}
  \texttt{CAN\_ID} = (\texttt{PACKET\_TYPE} \ll 8) \;|\; \texttt{ESC\_ID}
  \label{eq:canid}
\end{equation}

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{rlll}
\toprule
\textbf{PACKET\_TYPE} & \textbf{Constant name} & \textbf{Payload} &
\textbf{CAN\_ID (ESC=0x02)} \\
\midrule
0 & \texttt{SET\_DUTY}           & int32: duty $\times 10^5$    & \texttt{0x00000002} \\
1 & \texttt{SET\_CURRENT}        & int32: amps $\times 10^3$    & \texttt{0x00000102} \\
2 & \texttt{SET\_CURRENT\_BRAKE} & int32: amps $\times 10^3$    & \texttt{0x00000202} \\
3 & \texttt{SET\_RPM}            & int32: ERPM (raw)             & \texttt{0x00000302} \\
4 & \texttt{SET\_POS}            & int32: deg $\times 10^4$     & \texttt{0x00000402} \\
5 & \texttt{SET\_ORIGIN\_HERE}   & 1 byte: 0=volatile, 1=flash  & \texttt{0x00000502} \\
6 & \texttt{SET\_POS\_SPD}       & 8 bytes: pos+spd+acc         & \texttt{0x00000602} \\
8 & \texttt{SET\_MIT}            & 8 bytes bit-packed (\S\ref{sec:mit}) & \texttt{0x00000802} \\
\midrule
0x29 & \emph{Feedback (rx only)} & 8 bytes: pos+spd+I+T+err     & \texttt{0x00002902} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Constants defined in \texttt{src/ak\_v3\_common.py};
\texttt{src/ak\_v3\_can.py} calls \texttt{\_send\_eid(packet\_type, payload)}.

\subsection{R-Link CAN Mode setting}

Controls feedback delivery only -- not which command types are accepted.
MIT and servo commands work in either mode.

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{lp{9cm}}
\toprule
Setting & Behaviour \\
\midrule
\textbf{Periodic Feedback} & Motor broadcasts feedback at \texttt{CAN Fdb Rate}
                              (default 50\,Hz). Recommended for bench use. \\
\textbf{Inquiry Feedback}  & Motor replies with one frame per command received. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

% =========================================================================
\section{MIT force-control mode}
\label{sec:mit}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{Torque law}

The on-board controller implements:
\begin{equation}
  \tau_{\text{cmd}} =
    K_p\,(q_{\text{des}} - q)
  + K_d\,(\dot{q}_{\text{des}} - \dot{q})
  + \tau_{ff}
  \label{eq:mit}
\end{equation}

Sub-modes are selected by which parameters are nonzero:

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{llllll}
\toprule
Sub-mode & $K_p$ & $K_d$ & $q_{des}$ & $\dot{q}_{des}$ & $\tau_{ff}$ \\
\midrule
Position hold     & $> 0$ & $> 0$ & target & 0 & 0 \\
Velocity tracking & 0     & $> 0$ & 0      & target & 0 \\
Open-loop torque  & 0     & 0     & 0      & 0 & target \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Warning:} $K_p > 0$, $K_d = 0$ removes all damping -- motor
oscillates or runs away.  Always set $K_d \ge 0.3$ with position commands.

\subsection{Bit-packing (verbatim from \texttt{AK-V3.ino})}

Each float is linearly mapped to an unsigned integer:
\begin{align}
  \texttt{float\_to\_uint}(x) &= \left\lfloor
    \frac{x - x_{\min}}{x_{\max} - x_{\min}} \cdot (2^N - 1)
  \right\rfloor \label{eq:f2u} \\
  \texttt{uint\_to\_float}(u) &= \frac{u}{2^N - 1} \cdot (x_{\max} - x_{\min}) + x_{\min}
  \label{eq:u2f}
\end{align}

Bit widths and ranges for AK60-6 (from \texttt{src/ak\_v3\_common.py}):

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{llccc}
\toprule
Field & Symbol & Bits ($N$) & $x_{\min}$ & $x_{\max}$ \\
\midrule
Position      & $q$         & 16 & $-12.56$\,rad & $+12.56$\,rad \\
Velocity      & $\dot{q}$   & 12 & $-60$\,rad/s  & $+60$\,rad/s  \\
Stiffness     & $K_p$       & 12 & 0             & 500           \\
Damping       & $K_d$       & 12 & 0             & 5             \\
Feed-forward  & $\tau_{ff}$ & 12 & $-12$\,N$\cdot$m & $+12$\,N$\cdot$m \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{lstlisting}
# src/cubemars/ak_v3/ak_v3_common.py
def float_to_uint(x, x_min, x_max, bits):
    span = x_max - x_min
    x = max(x_min, min(x_max, x))
    return int((x - x_min) * ((1 << bits) - 1) / span)

def uint_to_float(u, x_min, x_max, bits):
    return u * (x_max - x_min) / ((1 << bits) - 1) + x_min
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{V3.0 MIT frame byte layout (Kp-first)}

V3.0 places $K_p$ in bytes 0--1 (\textbf{Kp-first}), unlike the classic
mini-cheetah layout (position-first) used by earlier T-Motor firmware:

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
Byte & Content \\
\midrule
0 & \texttt{Kp[11:4]} \\
1 & \texttt{Kp[3:0] | Kd[11:8]} \\
2 & \texttt{Kd[7:0]} \\
3 & \texttt{pos[15:8]} \\
4 & \texttt{pos[7:0]} \\
5 & \texttt{vel[11:4]} \\
6 & \texttt{vel[3:0] | tau[11:8]} \\
7 & \texttt{tau[7:0]} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Sent at extended CAN ID $(8 \ll 8) | \texttt{0x02} = \texttt{0x0802}$.
Implemented in \texttt{CubeMarsAkV3Motor.set\_mit()}:

\begin{lstlisting}
def set_mit(self, p_des, v_des, kp, kd, t_ff):
    L = self.limits
    p_int  = float_to_uint(p_des, L.p_min,  L.p_max,  16)
    v_int  = float_to_uint(v_des, L.v_min,  L.v_max,  12)
    kp_int = float_to_uint(kp,    0.0,      L.kp_max, 12)
    kd_int = float_to_uint(kd,    0.0,      L.kd_max, 12)
    t_int  = float_to_uint(t_ff,  L.t_min,  L.t_max,  12)
    buf = bytes([
        (kp_int >> 4) & 0xFF,
        ((kp_int & 0xF) << 4) | ((kd_int >> 8) & 0xF),
        kd_int & 0xFF,
        (p_int >> 8) & 0xFF,
        p_int & 0xFF,
        (v_int >> 4) & 0xFF,
        ((v_int & 0xF) << 4) | ((t_int >> 8) & 0xF),
        t_int & 0xFF,
    ])
    self._send_eid(CAN_PACKET_SET_MIT, buf)
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Feedback frame (CAN ID \texttt{0x2902})}

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{cll}
\toprule
Bytes & Encoding & Meaning \\
\midrule
0--1 & int16 BE, $\times 0.1$ & Position (degrees) \\
2--3 & int16 BE, $\times 10$  & Speed (ERPM) \\
4--5 & int16 BE, $\times 0.01$ & Phase current $I_q$ (A) \\
6    & int8  & Driver-board MOS temperature (\textdegree C) \\
7    & uint8 & Error code (0 = no fault) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Decoded in \texttt{CubeMarsAkV3Motor.parse\_feedback()}:

\begin{lstlisting}
def parse_feedback(self, msg):
    if msg.arbitration_id != (FEEDBACK_PACKET_TYPE << 8) | self.can_id:
        return False
    d = msg.data
    self.pos_deg   = int.from_bytes(d[0:2], "big", signed=True) * 0.1
    self.spd_erpm  = int.from_bytes(d[2:4], "big", signed=True) * 10.0
    self.current_a = int.from_bytes(d[4:6], "big", signed=True) * 0.01
    self.temp_c    = d[6]
    self.error     = d[7]
    return True
\end{lstlisting}

\texttt{CubeMarsAkV3Bench.read\_state()} converts to SI units:
\begin{align}
  q_{\text{rad}} &= q_{\deg} \cdot \tfrac{\pi}{180} \label{eq:deg2rad} \\
  \dot{q}_{\text{rad/s}} &= \frac{\text{ERPM}}{p_\text{pairs}} \cdot \frac{2\pi}{60}
  \qquad (p_\text{pairs} = 14 \text{ for AK60-6})
  \label{eq:erpm2rads}
\end{align}

\section{Serial UART protocol and parameter read}
\label{sec:serial}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{Frame format (\S4.3.2 of the V3.0.1 manual)}

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
Byte(s) & Content \\
\midrule
0       & Header \texttt{0xAA} \\
1       & Data length $N$ (command byte + payload) \\
2       & Command byte (\texttt{comm\_id}) \\
3..N+1  & Payload bytes \\
N+2     & CRC16 high byte \\
N+3     & CRC16 low byte \\
N+4     & Tail \texttt{0xBB} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

CRC-16 over bytes 2..(N+1) using CCITT polynomial table (\S4.3.2.3).
Verification: \texttt{crc16([0x45])} $= \texttt{0x1861}$, giving the
full \texttt{COMM\_GET\_VALUES} request: \texttt{AA 01 45 18 61 BB}.

\subsection{\texttt{COMM\_GET\_VALUES} (0x45) -- full status reply}

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{cllr}

\midrule

1--2  & int16 BE    & MOS temperature     & $\div 10$\,\textdegree C \\

\begin{lstlisting}

# Native units -- pos (deg), ERPM, ia (A), temp (C), error code:
pos, erpm, ia, tc, err = bus.read_raw()["j1"]
\end{lstlisting}

\texttt{read\_state()} sends one zero-torque MIT frame first to elicit a
fresh feedback reply (in Inquiry-Feedback mode), then polls
\texttt{poll\_feedback()}.

\paragraph{Converting $I_q$ to torque:}
$\tau\,[\text{N$\cdot$m}] = I_q\,[\text{A}] \times K_t\,[\text{N$\cdot$m/A}]$
where $K_t \approx 0.12$\,N$\cdot$m/A for AK60-6 KV80
(from R-Link Motor Identification result).

\section{MIT control modes -- theory and recommended gains}
\label{sec:control}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{Position hold}

From Eq.~\eqref{eq:mit} with $\dot{q}_{\text{des}} = 0$:
$\tau = K_p(q_{\text{des}} - q) - K_d\dot{q}$.
Motor acts as a spring ($K_p$) with viscous damping ($K_d$).

\begin{lstlisting}
bus.write("goal_position", {"j1": math.radians(30)}, kp=60, kd=1.5)
q, dq, ia = bus.read_state()["j1"]
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Velocity tracking}

$K_p = 0$, $K_d > 0$: $\tau = K_d(\dot{q}_{\text{des}} - \dot{q})$.
No position restoring force.

\begin{lstlisting}
bus.write("goal_velocity", {"j1": 1.0}, kd=1.5)   # 1 rad/s
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Open-loop torque}

$K_p = K_d = 0$: $\tau_{\text{cmd}} = \tau_{ff}$.  No feedback loop.

\begin{lstlisting}
bus.write("goal_torque", {"j1": 0.3})   # 0.3 N.m
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Recommended gains}

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
Task & $K_p$ & $K_d$ & Notes \\
\midrule
Stiff position hold  & 80--150 & 2.0--4.0  & Heavy load; tune $K_d$ first \\
Moderate hold        & 30--60  & 1.0--2.0  & General-purpose bench \\

\section{Impedance control and kinesthetic teaching}
\label{sec:impedance}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{Back-drivable operation}

With low $K_p$ and $K_d$, setting $q_{\text{des}} = q_{\text{meas}}$
each tick keeps the spring force zero:
\[
  \tau_{\text{spring}} = K_p\underbrace{(q_{\text{des}} - q)}_{=\,0} = 0
\]
Only the damping term $-K_d\dot{q}$ resists fast movements.
Lower $K_d$ = softer feel; too low causes jitter from encoder noise.

\subsection{Recording a demonstration (\texttt{05\_kinesthetic\_record.py})}

\begin{lstlisting}
KP, KD = 5.0, 0.4   # soft: compliant but not jittery
with open_cubemars_ak_v3_bench(set_zero=True) as bus:
    while True:
        q, dq, ia = bus.read_state(timeout=0.02)["j1"]
        bus.write("goal_position", {"j1": q}, kp=KP, kd=KD)
        rows.append((time.monotonic(), q, dq, ia))
\end{lstlisting}

Saves to \texttt{demo\_trajectory\_ak80.csv}:
columns \texttt{t\_s, q\_rad, dq\_rad\_s, current\_a} at 100\,Hz.

\subsection{Replaying a demonstration (\texttt{06\_replay\_trajectory.py})}

Use higher gains during replay for tracking under load:
\begin{lstlisting}
bus.write("goal_position", {"j1": q_des[i]}, kp=30, kd=1.0)
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Spring-return impedance (\texttt{07\_impedance\_spring\_return.py})}

Fixed anchor at $q = 0$; motor springs back when released:
\begin{lstlisting}
# 30-second loop at 100 Hz
bus.write("goal_position", {"j1": 0.0}, kp=10, kd=0.5)
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Calibration (R-Link, one time)}
\label{sec:cal}
% =========================================================================

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Motor Identification} -- R-Link spins the motor to measure
        resistance, inductance, and back-EMF.  Motor must be free to spin.
  \item \textbf{Encoder Identification} -- samples encoder at many rotor
        positions; builds correction table.  Run immediately after Motor ID.
  \item \textbf{Write Parameters} -- click \emph{Write}.  Save
        \texttt{.AppParams} and \texttt{.McParams} as backups.
  \item \textbf{CAN settings} -- set CAN Bitrate = 1000\,kbps,
        CAN ID = 2, CAN Mode = Periodic Feedback.
\end{enumerate}

Re-calibrate after swapping motor phase wires, replacing the motor, or
flashing new firmware.

\paragraph{Software zero (volatile):}
\begin{lstlisting}
with open_cubemars_ak_v3_bench(set_zero=True) as bus: ...
# or explicitly after connect:
bus.set_zero()   # permanent=False by default (RAM only, resets on power)
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Known issues and gotchas}
\label{sec:gotchas}
% =========================================================================

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Never Kd\,=\,0 in position mode.}
  $K_p > 0$, $K_d = 0$ removes all damping; motor oscillates or runs away.
  Always set $K_d \ge 0.3$ with position commands.

\item \textbf{ESC\_ID = 0x02.}
  Wrong ESC\_ID causes \texttt{read\_state()} to return cached zeros
  silently.  Verify with \texttt{00\_probe\_motor.py}; change via R-Link.

\item \textbf{can1 missing after reboot.}
  Replug the DSDTech adapter (udev rule brings \texttt{can1} up), or:
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
sudo ip link set can1 up type can bitrate 1000000
\end{lstlisting}

\item \textbf{gs\_usb ``Unexpected unused echo id'' in dmesg.}
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
sudo rmmod gs_usb && sudo modprobe gs_usb
\end{lstlisting}

\item \textbf{read\_state() tau field is amps, not N$\cdot$m.}
  Third element \texttt{ia} of \texttt{(q, dq, ia)} is $I_q$ in amps.
  Multiply by $K_t \approx 0.12$\,N$\cdot$m/A for torque.

\item \textbf{V3.0 ignores 11-bit standard CAN frames.}
  The legacy \texttt{cubemars\_bus.py} (HDSC serial transport) has been
  removed from the repo -- it silently failed on V3.0 firmware.
  Use \texttt{cubemars/ak\_v3/AK\_V3\_CAN.py} / \texttt{CubeMarsAkV3Bench} exclusively.

\item \textbf{Calibration required before first use.}
  An uncalibrated motor may spin erratically.  Run R-Link Motor ID +
  Encoder ID (Section~\ref{sec:cal}).

\item \textbf{Peak current caution.}
  Motor allows up to 30\,A peak.  Keep $\tau_{ff} \le 1$\,N$\cdot$m when
  unloaded (\texttt{04\_mit\_torque.py}).

\item \textbf{00\_read\_params.py requires a USB-UART adapter.}
  The DSDTech SH-C30A is a CAN-only adapter.  Use a separate FTDI/CH340
  dongle wired to the motor's 3-pin UART terminal.

\end{enumerate}

\section{Quick-start checklist}
\label{sec:checklist}
% =========================================================================

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Power on motor (18--52\,V).
  \item Plug DSDTech SH-C30A into Jetson USB.  Confirm:
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
ip -details link show can1
\end{lstlisting}
  \item Passive sniff -- confirm motor broadcasting:
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
sudo python3 tests/cubemars/00_sniff_motor.py
\end{lstlisting}
  Expected: frames with CAN ID \texttt{0x2902} at 50\,Hz.
  \item Probe motor -- verify ESC\_ID and round-trip:
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
sudo python3 tests/cubemars/00_probe_motor.py
\end{lstlisting}
  \item Connection check -- 5 frames, non-zero values:
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
sudo python3 tests/cubemars/01_check_connection.py
\end{lstlisting}
  \item MIT position sweep:
\begin{lstlisting}[numbers=none]
sudo python3 tests/cubemars/02_mit_position.py
\end{lstlisting}
  \item Proceed with scripts \texttt{03}--\texttt{07} as needed.
\end{enumerate}
